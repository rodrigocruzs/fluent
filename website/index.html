The real gap

It’s not your English. It’s that no one can tell you what to fix.

You’re fluent enough to do the job. The gap is smaller and far more specific than “get better at English” — and it shows up in the same handful of moments, every single week.

01

The hedge pile

“I just wanted to maybe quickly check if possibly we could…” By the time you reach the point, the room has already decided it’s optional.

02

“Be clearer.”

The most common note non-native speakers get in reviews — and the least useful one. Clearer how? Which sentence lost them? Nobody ever says.

03

Lost on the way out

You had the sharp version in your head. In English it came out three clauses deep, and the actual point got buried before anyone heard it.

04

The filler fog

“Um, so, like, you know…” The filler you stop hearing in yourself is the first thing others use to decide how sure you sound.

How it works

It works like this.

Fluent runs in the background during your real calls. Afterward it shows you specific moments — what you actually said, what it noticed, and what to try next time. No staged exercises. No scripts. Your own words.

WHAT YOU SAID

“Um, I think maybe we should possibly consider pushing the deadline, if that’s okay with everyone?”

WHAT FLUENT NOTICED

Four hedges stacked in one sentence — “think,” “maybe,” “possibly,” “if that’s okay.” Your recommendation arrived sounding like a request for permission.

WHAT IT COACHED TOWARD

“I recommend we push the deadline. Here’s why.” — state the call, then support it.

WHAT YOU SAID

“So yeah, the thing is, like, the data is kind of showing us that, you know, engagement is down a bit.”

WHAT FLUENT NOTICED

Six filler words before the actual point. Listeners had started tuning out before “engagement is down” ever landed.

WHAT IT COACHED TOWARD

Lead with the headline: “Engagement dropped 12% this month.” Put the softeners after, if at all.

WHAT YOU SAID

“I’m not sure if this is right, but I was thinking we could maybe try a different approach?”

WHAT FLUENT NOTICED

You opened by undercutting yourself. The idea was strong — the framing told the room to doubt it before hearing it.

WHAT IT COACHED TOWARD

“Here’s a different approach I’d try —” Let the idea stand on its own before you qualify it.
